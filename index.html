Hedy Lamarr

The iconic Hollywood actress, also made groundbreaking contributions to IT with her co-invention of frequency hopping, a precursor to modern Wi-Fi and Bluetooth technology.

Technological timeline

  • 1941
    Co-invented frequency hopping technology with composer George Antheil as a means to secure radio communications during World War II.
  • 1962
    Patent granted for their frequency hopping spread spectrum invention, although its significance in modern technology wasn't fully realized at the time.
  • 1980s
    Recognition of Lamarr and Antheil's invention grows as it becomes foundational to modern communication technologies, including Wi-Fi and Bluetooth.
  • 1997
    Lamarr and Antheil were honored with the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award for their pioneering work in frequency hopping technology.
  • Posthumous Legacy
    Lamarr is celebrated as a visionary inventor whose contributions to IT continue to influence and shape modern communication technologies.
